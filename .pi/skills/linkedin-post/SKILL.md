# LinkedIn Post (Engineering Leadership)

## Voice

- Witty but professional — sharp observations delivered with personality
- Short and punchy: aim for 150–250 words
- Emojis for strong emphasis only (1–3 per post, never decorative)
- No humble bragging, no overly polished language, no `--`

## Post Anatomy

Every post needs all four parts:

1. **Hook** — first line stops the scroll (see [Hook Formulas](#hook-formulas))
2. **Body** — tension, value, or story in short punchy lines
3. **Close** — bold insight or unexpected turn
4. **CTA** — share-focused to build network visibility

## Hook Formulas

Pick one per post:

- **Contrarian**: "Most engineering managers get X completely wrong."
- **Uncomfortable truth**: "Nobody talks about how [relatable EM pain]."
- **Bold headline**: One punchy sentence that sounds like a news alert.
- **Number + promise**: "3 things I wish I knew before my first 1:1."
- **Tension setup**: A short scenario the reader instantly recognizes.

## Post Formats

- **Hot take** — bold statement + why + invite disagreement
- **Numbered tips** — 3–5 actionable items, one line each
- **Story → lesson** — 3–4 line micro-story, one-line takeaway
- **Before/after** — "Old me: X. Now: Y."
- **"Nobody tells you..."** — relatable tension list

## The 5 Pillars (apply at least 2 per post)

1. 😅 **Relatable tension** — say what EMs feel but don't admit
2. 👩‍💻 **Actionable value** — something they can use today, for free
3. 💅 **Personality** — wit, a small story, a quirk
4. 👀 **Bold statement** — something worth arguing about
5. 🫶 **Community cue** — tag, involve, or invite the reader

## CTA Patterns (share-focused)

- "Save this for your next 1:1."
- "Repost if your team needs to hear this."
- "Tag an EM who's been here."
- "Which one resonates? Drop it below."

## Workflow

1. Get the topic or raw thought from the user
2. Pick the best format + hook formula for it
3. Draft using the 5 pillars (at least 2 per post)
4. Apply voice rules: punchy lines, strategic emojis, no `--`
5. End with a share-focused CTA
6. Present draft and offer to adjust tone, length, or hook
