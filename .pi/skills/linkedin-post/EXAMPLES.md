# LinkedIn Post Examples

Three reference posts showing each core format. Use these to calibrate tone and length.

---

## Format: Hot Take

**Topic:** Engineering managers avoid giving real feedback

---

Most engineering managers don't have a feedback problem.

They have a discomfort problem.

The feedback exists. The words are there. But somewhere between the thought and the 1:1, it gets softened into noise.

"You're doing great, just... maybe consider being a bit more proactive?"

That's not feedback. That's a riddle.

Your team doesn't need you to be nice. They need you to be clear. 🎯

Repost if your team deserves better than that.

---

**Pillars used:** Relatable tension, bold statement, community cue
**Hook type:** Contrarian

---

## Format: Numbered Tips

**Topic:** Building trust with your engineers

---

3 things that will make your engineers trust you more 👇

1. Say "I don't know" out loud. In the meeting. On the call.
2. Fight for their priorities in rooms they're not in.
3. Give feedback the same day, not in the next sprint retro.

None of these require a framework.

Just consistency.

Tag an EM who does all three. 🙌

---

**Pillars used:** Actionable value, community cue, personality
**Hook type:** Number + promise

---

## Format: Story → Lesson

**Topic:** Team problems are rarely about process

---

I once spent two weeks trying to fix a team's velocity.

Standups. Retros. Ticket breakdowns. More process.

Nothing worked.

Turns out one engineer hadn't spoken to their skip-level in six months and assumed they were getting laid off.

Sometimes the bottleneck isn't the process.

It's the silence. 👀

Save this for your next skip-level. It might be overdue.

---

**Pillars used:** Relatable tension, personality, actionable value
**Hook type:** Tension setup
